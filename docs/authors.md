# Authors and Key Facts

This page is the fast-reference map for who each thinker is, what they changed, and why they still matter.

## Classical Antiquity

### Plato
- Key facts: Student of Socrates; founded the Academy in Athens.
- What changed: Built systematic dialectical philosophy around justice, truth, and political order.
- Main ideas: Forms, philosopher-ruler, tripartite soul.
- Lasting impact: Core foundations for metaphysics, political theory, and moral psychology.

### Aristotle
- Key facts: Student of Plato; founded the Lyceum; tutor to Alexander.
- What changed: Turned philosophy toward formal logic and empirical explanation.
- Main ideas: Four causes, virtue ethics, substance/form.
- Lasting impact: Deep influence on science, ethics, and medieval philosophy.

### Confucius
- Key facts: Teacher from Lu; sayings preserved in the *Analects*.
- What changed: Centered ethics in social roles, ritual, and self-cultivation.
- Main ideas: Ren, li, junzi.
- Lasting impact: Major influence on East Asian ethics, politics, and education.

### Epicurus
- Key facts: Founded The Garden in Athens.
- What changed: Reframed pleasure as tranquility and freedom from fear.
- Main ideas: Ataraxia, simple desires, mortality without terror.
- Lasting impact: Practical ethics of well-being and desire management.

### Epictetus
- Key facts: Born enslaved, later became a Stoic teacher.
- What changed: Popularized the control/not-control discipline.
- Main ideas: Inner freedom, disciplined judgment, agency.
- Lasting impact: Practical resilience models and cognitive self-management.

### Marcus Aurelius
- Key facts: Roman emperor writing private Stoic reflections.
- What changed: Exemplified philosophy as daily practice under pressure.
- Main ideas: Duty, impermanence, discipline.
- Lasting impact: Leadership ethics and applied Stoicism.

### Plotinus
- Key facts: Central Neoplatonist thinker; *Enneads* compiled by Porphyry.
- What changed: Hierarchical metaphysics from The One to intellect to soul.
- Main ideas: Emanation, return, contemplation.
- Lasting impact: Shaped late antique and medieval metaphysical traditions.

## Late Antiquity and Medieval

### Augustine of Hippo
- Key facts: North African bishop and major Christian philosopher.
- What changed: United introspection, theology, and classical philosophy.
- Main ideas: Grace, memory/time, interiority.
- Lasting impact: Foundation for Western theology and philosophy of self.

### Avicenna (Ibn Sina)
- Key facts: Persian polymath in medicine, logic, and metaphysics.
- What changed: Clarified essence/existence and necessity in metaphysics.
- Main ideas: Necessary Existent, demonstrative science, floating man.
- Lasting impact: Core reference point for Islamic and scholastic thought.

### Maimonides
- Key facts: Jewish legal scholar, philosopher, and physician.
- What changed: Reconciled Aristotelian reason with scriptural tradition.
- Main ideas: Negative theology, law and rational inquiry.
- Lasting impact: Central figure in Jewish philosophy and interfaith medieval debates.

### Thomas Aquinas
- Key facts: Dominican scholar and major system-builder.
- What changed: Integrated Aristotelian philosophy into Christian theology.
- Main ideas: Natural law, analogy, faith/reason complementarity.
- Lasting impact: Durable influence on ethics, theology, and legal thought.

## Early Modern

### Rene Descartes
- Key facts: Mathematician-philosopher and method-focused skeptic.
- What changed: Recentered philosophy around epistemic certainty.
- Main ideas: Cogito, clear/distinct ideas, dualism.
- Lasting impact: Opened modern epistemology and philosophy of mind.

### Thomas Hobbes
- Key facts: Political philosopher shaped by civil war context.
- What changed: Grounded political authority in social contract and security.
- Main ideas: State of nature, sovereign power.
- Lasting impact: Core source for modern political realism.

### Baruch Spinoza
- Key facts: Excommunicated thinker and independent scholar.
- What changed: Proposed strict substance monism.
- Main ideas: God/Nature unity, conatus, rational affect transformation.
- Lasting impact: Influenced modern secularism and naturalism.

### John Locke
- Key facts: Physician and liberal political theorist.
- What changed: Defended empiricism and consent-based legitimacy.
- Main ideas: Tabula rasa, rights, property.
- Lasting impact: Liberal constitutional and educational traditions.

### David Hume
- Key facts: Scottish Enlightenment skeptic.
- What changed: Critiqued causation certainty and rational overreach.
- Main ideas: Habit, induction problem, is/ought gap.
- Lasting impact: Pushed modern philosophy toward critical empiricism.

## Enlightenment and German Idealism

### Jean-Jacques Rousseau
- Key facts: Genevan critic of inequality and corruption.
- What changed: Reframed freedom as collective self-legislation.
- Main ideas: General will, civic education.
- Lasting impact: Republican and democratic political theory.

### Immanuel Kant
- Key facts: Konigsberg professor; major synthesis thinker.
- What changed: Joined rationalism/empiricism under transcendental method.
- Main ideas: Autonomy, categorical imperative, conditions of experience.
- Lasting impact: Modern ethics, epistemology, and aesthetics architecture.

### G.W.F. Hegel
- Key facts: German idealist focused on history and institutions.
- What changed: Made historical development central to reason.
- Main ideas: Dialectic, recognition, ethical life.
- Lasting impact: Influenced Marxism, existentialism, critical theory.

### Soren Kierkegaard
- Key facts: Danish existential precursor, often pseudonymous.
- What changed: Shifted focus from systems to lived commitment.
- Main ideas: Anxiety, faith, existential choice.
- Lasting impact: Existentialism and theology of subjectivity.

### John Stuart Mill
- Key facts: Utilitarian reformer and parliamentarian.
- What changed: Defended liberty against both state and social pressure.
- Main ideas: Harm principle, individuality, quality of pleasures.
- Lasting impact: Liberal rights and free speech discourse.

### Karl Marx
- Key facts: Philosopher-economist of industrial capitalism.
- What changed: Linked social theory to material production structures.
- Main ideas: Class conflict, alienation, surplus value.
- Lasting impact: Global labor, economic, and political critique traditions.

## Late Modern and Analytic

### Friedrich Nietzsche
- Key facts: Philologist turned cultural critic.
- What changed: Historicized morality and challenged inherited values.
- Main ideas: Genealogy, nihilism, value revaluation.
- Lasting impact: Major source for existential and post-structural thought.

### Gottlob Frege
- Key facts: Logician who transformed formal semantics.
- What changed: Built modern predicate logic and meaning distinctions.
- Main ideas: Sense/reference, anti-psychologism.
- Lasting impact: Foundations of analytic philosophy and logic.

### Bertrand Russell
- Key facts: Logician, public intellectual, Nobel laureate.
- What changed: Advanced logical analysis as method.
- Main ideas: Descriptions theory, analytic decomposition.
- Lasting impact: Canonical analytic philosophy framework.

### Ludwig Wittgenstein
- Key facts: Philosopher with early and late major phases.
- What changed: Shifted from logical form to language-in-use.
- Main ideas: Language games, forms of life, meaning as use.
- Lasting impact: Reframed philosophy of language and method.
